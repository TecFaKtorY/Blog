Employment/Enrollment Update: How Fake Recruiters Steal Identities

Cyber Security | Tec Faktory | July 2026
Educational Disclaimer

This article is for educational purposes only. It is created to help people recognize and defend themselves against online scams. Tec Faktory does not support or encourage fraudulent activities.

The Scenario

A recruiter posts a job advertisement for a $100/hr remote position that promises to improve your life by simply copying and pasting information.

  • They provide a website where you register formally.
  • They tell you your account will be monitored because company funds may pass through it.
  • They request banking details.
  • They claim they need to inspect your account.

After some time, the victim discovers unknown expenses, transfers, or loans connected to their identity. The recruiter disappears and the victim is left with the consequences.

What Really Happens Behind The Scenes

Scammers advertise fake jobs on platforms such as:

  • Indeed
  • LinkedIn Jobs
  • Glassdoor
  • ZipRecruiter
  • Monster Jobs
  • CareerBuilder
  • SimplyHired
  • Social media job groups
WORK FROM HOME - $100/HR

Job: Copy & Paste / Data Analyst

Requirements:
- Live in USA
- Speak good English
- Comfortable with smartphone/computer

Apply: send mail to :sample@gmail.com to complete application.

Some scammers even use AI-generated videos, lip-sync videos, and advertisements to make fake opportunities appear real.

The Conversation

Hi, welcome to {company name} recruitment portal. We hope you have reviewed and meet our requirements.
Good, kindly proceed through our registration portal before further conversation: www.[companywebsite].com
Congratulations on your registration! Kindly wait while we verify your request. This process helps confirm you are not a bot and can be trusted with company funds.

The Hidden Stage

After collecting personal information, scammers may involve other criminals (often referred to as Loader) and prepare the next stage of the fraud. The victim believes they are completing employment verification, but their information would be used to perform explicit acts without their clear knowledge.

Verification successful!

Kindly provide your bank details so we can send your starter funds. please note that up to $100,000 may be allocated for equipment and company expenses (for other departments).

This is when the background work begins, they begin to run all forms of method getting loans etc in your name.

How To Protect Yourself

  • Research companies before accepting offers.
  • Never provide banking details to unknown recruiters.
  • Verify job offers through official channels.
  • Be careful of unrealistic salaries.
  • Avoid pressure tactics.

Final Thoughts

Scammers often target people searching for better opportunities. Awareness is one of the strongest ways to protect yourself.

Stay informed. Stay secure. Stay protected.

Employment Scam Alert: How Fake Recruiters Can Steal Your Identity

Cyber Security | Tec Faktory | July 2026
Disclaimer:

This article is for educational and awareness purposes only. It is created to help people recognize and avoid online employment scams. Tec Faktory does not support or encourage illegal activities.

The Scenario

Imagine seeing a job advertisement promising $100 per hour for simple tasks such as copying and pasting information. The recruiter claims it is an easy remote job that could change your life.

The process usually looks convincing:

  • You are given a website where you are asked to register.
  • The recruiter tells you your account will be monitored because company funds may pass through it.
  • You are asked to provide your banking details.
  • They claim your account is being verified.

After some time, victims may discover unauthorized transactions, unexpected expenses, or financial activities they never approved. The recruiter disappears, emails stop working, and the victim is left dealing with the consequences.

What Really Happens Behind The Scenes

Scammers often advertise fake jobs on job platforms and social media groups.

  • Indeed
  • LinkedIn Jobs
  • Glassdoor
  • ZipRecruiter
  • Facebook job groups
  • WhatsApp and Telegram groups

A fake job post may look like this:

Work From Home - Earn $100/Hour

Position: Copy & Paste, Data Entry, Data Analyst

Requirements:
- Good English communication
- Smartphone or computer access
- Ability to work remotely

The Conversation Usually Starts Like This

Hi, welcome to our recruitment portal. We hope you meet all our requirements for this position.
Kindly complete your registration on our company portal before we proceed.
Congratulations! Your registration has been received. Please wait while we verify your account to ensure you can be trusted with company funds.

Why They Ask For Your Bank Details

The explanation may sound professional, but legitimate employers rarely request sensitive financial information before completing a proper hiring process.

Scammers may attempt to:

  • Steal personal information.
  • Commit identity fraud.
  • Apply for loans using stolen information.
  • Use accounts for illegal financial activities.

How To Protect Yourself

  • Research the company before accepting any offer.
  • Visit official websites independently.
  • Verify recruiters through official channels.
  • Never share banking details with unknown employers.
  • Be careful of offers that seem too good to be true.

Final Thoughts

Scammers often target people searching for better opportunities. Awareness is one of the strongest ways to protect yourself.

Before trusting any online opportunity, take time to verify it. A few minutes of research can save you from serious financial problems.

Stay informed. Stay secure. Stay protected.

Employment/Enrollment Update: How Fake Recruiters Steal Identities

Cyber Security | Tec Faktory | July 2026
Educational Disclaimer:

This article is created for educational purposes only. The goal is to help people identify and defend themselves against online scams. Tec Faktory does not support or encourage fraudulent activities.

The Scenario

A recruiter posts a job advertisement for a $100/hr remote position that promises to improve your life by simply copying and pasting information.

The process appears professional:

  • They provide a website where you register formally.
  • They inform you that your account will be monitored because company funds may pass through it.
  • They request banking details.
  • They claim they need to inspect or verify your account.

After some time, the victim discovers unknown expenses, transfers, or loans connected to their identity. The recruiter disappears, emails stop receiving replies, and the victim is left dealing with the consequences.

What Really Happens Behind The Scenes

The scammer creates fake job advertisements and posts them on platforms such as:

  • Indeed
  • LinkedIn Jobs
  • Glassdoor
  • ZipRecruiter
  • Monster Jobs
  • CareerBuilder
  • SimplyHired
  • Social media job groups
WORK FROM HOME - $100/HR

Job: Copy & Paste / Data Analyst

Requirements:
- Live in USA
- Speak good English
- Be comfortable with smartphone/computer

Apply through: sample@gmail.com

Some scammers also use AI-generated videos, lip-sync videos, and paid advertisements to appear more legitimate and attract more victims.

The Conversation

Hi, welcome to {company name} recruitment portal. We hope you have reviewed and meet our requirements.
Good, kindly proceed through our registration portal before any further conversation: www.[companywebsite].com
Congratulations on your registration. Kindly wait while we verify your request. This process helps us confirm you are not a bot and that you can be trusted with company funds.

The Hidden Stage

After collecting personal information, scammers may involve other criminals and prepare the next stage of the fraud. The victim believes they are progressing through employment verification, but the information provided may be used for illegal financial activities.

Verification successful!

To proceed, kindly provide your bank details so we can send your starter funds. Up to $100,000 may be allocated for equipment and company expenses.

What Happens Next?

This is where victims may begin experiencing unauthorized financial activities carried out using their identity.

  • Identity theft attempts.
  • Unauthorized financial applications.
  • Fraudulent transactions.
  • Other illegal activities performed using stolen information.

How To Protect Yourself

  • Research companies before accepting offers.
  • Never provide banking details to unknown recruiters.
  • Verify job offers through official company channels.
  • Be careful with unrealistic salaries.
  • Avoid pressure tactics.

Final Thoughts

I hope you have learned a lot from this post and it helps you become more aware of scammers and fraudsters. Do not allow desperation or greed to make you ignore warning signs.

Stay informed. Stay secure. Stay protected.

What Is Stock Investing?

Stock investing means buying shares of a company. When you own a share, you own a small portion of that business and may benefit if the company's value grows over time.

Some companies also pay dividends, allowing shareholders to receive a portion of the company's profits.

Did You Know?

Many successful investors build wealth gradually by investing consistently over several years rather than trying to get rich overnight.

Why Do People Invest?

People invest in stocks for different reasons, including:

  • Growing their wealth over time.
  • Saving for retirement.
  • Generating passive income through dividends.
  • Protecting money against inflation.
  • Building long-term financial security.

Common Investment Scams

Stay Alert

Fraudsters often target new investors through:

  • Guaranteed profit schemes.
  • Fake investment websites.
  • Impersonation of legitimate investment companies.
  • WhatsApp and Telegram investment groups.
  • Pressure to invest immediately.
  • Promises of doubling your money within days.

If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.
